Built different

A block-lattice, not a blockchain.

Every Nano account has its own chain. Transactions happen in parallel across accounts instead of one global queue — so the network scales horizontally without block-size wars, mempools, or congestion fees.

Parallel by design

Independent account chains let the network process thousands of transactions concurrently instead of serially.

No block size, no block time

Capacity grows with bandwidth. There is no arbitrary cap to auction off, so there are no fees to bid.

Most energy-efficient currency

No mining, no staking races. A Nano transaction costs about 0.111 Wh — orders of magnitude less than Visa, Solana, ACH, and Bitcoin. See the chart below.

Energy

The most energy-efficient currency in the world.

Because there's no mining and no race to produce blocks, the entire network only spends what it takes to relay a tiny vote. Per transaction, Nano uses less energy than your light switch does in the moment you flip it.

Open Representative Voting

Decentralized by every holder, every day.

There are no miners and no stakers earning yield. Instead, every account delegates its voting weight to a Representative of its choice — and can switch in one transaction, for free.
